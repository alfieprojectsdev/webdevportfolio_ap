About Me

I used to measure the shape of the Earth for a living. Now I build web apps. The transition makes more sense than you'd think — both involve precision, spatial thinking, and a high tolerance for finicky coordinate systems (hello, CSS Grid).

My development philosophy is simple: build things that solve actual problems for actual people. Not moonshot startups or blockchain-powered nonsense. Just practical tools for parking, lab bookings, carpools, and math worksheets — the unglamorous stuff that makes daily life smoother.

I believe in:

  • Test-driven development — If it's not tested, it's not done (sorry, optimists)
  • Systematic problem-solving — Break big problems into small ones, then solve the small ones
  • Clear documentation — Future-you will thank present-you for writing things down
  • Practical solutions — Technology should serve people, not impress other developers

When I'm not coding, I'm either helping my kids with homework or automating something that definitely didn't need automation (but where's the fun in that?).
