About Me

I used to work with GNSS data to study how the Earth shifts — during earthquakes and in the quiet, gradual movements between them. Now I build web apps, but the mindset’s the same: precision, spatial reasoning, and an appreciation for complex systems (looking at you, CSS Grid).

My philosophy is simple: build things that solve real problems for real people. No moonshot hype or blockchain detours — just thoughtful tools that make everyday work a little smoother.

I believe in:

  • Test-driven development — if it’s not tested, it’s not done
  • Systematic problem-solving — big problems get smaller when you break them right
  • Clear documentation — your future self deserves clarity
  • Practical design — tech should help people, not just impress developers

When I’m not coding, I’m helping my kids with homework or automating something that probably didn’t need automating — because curiosity doesn’t switch off after work.
